I am driven by a single, persistent question — why do we think the way we do, and what does understanding this process reveal about existence itself?

I have moved between two worlds for as long as I can remember — the world of Dostoevsky and Kafka, of Kant and Nietzsche, of surrealist canvases and philosophical margins filled with pencilled questions, and the world of logic, pattern, and computation. What I found, eventually, was that these were not two worlds at all. They were the same inquiry, approached from different directions.

I am a Bangladeshi national currently completing a Diploma in Computer Science and Engineering at Brainware University, Kolkata — with an SGPA of 9.70, a progression from 8.75 in my first semester after returning to academics following surgery and recovery. I am an active student researcher under Dr. Riya Sil on graph neural networks for overlap-aware speaker diarization, first author on a fuzzy-guided deep learning paper for diabetic retinopathy detection presented at SoCTA 2025, contributor to three book chapters across smart-city ethics, cybersecurity, and precision cardiology, and an invited peer reviewer for IEEE SPCOM 2026 at IISc Bangalore.

What sits beneath the technical work is older. Kant argued that the structure of the mind itself determines what mind can perceive. Heisenberg showed that observation is never neutral — that the act of measuring disturbs the measured. The boundary between knower and known is not a wall but a conversation. Any honest attempt to model intelligence must begin by acknowledging what it structurally cannot see.

The next frontier in AI is not building systems that compute more — but systems that genuinely represent uncertainty about what they do not know.

JARVIS-I — A 3D Mind-Map for Scattered Minds

Live projectAI · 3D VisualisationBuilt for ADHD & non-linear thinkers
+
Problem People whose thoughts arrive in fragments — those with ADHD, those who simply think in branches rather than lines — are poorly served by linear note-taking tools. Lists flatten what is meant to be a constellation. Mind-mapping apps help but are often visually cramped, and they ask the user to do the work of organising before they have understood what they are organising. Approach JARVIS-I is an AI-assisted, three-dimensional mind-map. The user pours thoughts in as they come; the AI infers structure, surfaces relationships, and renders the result as a navigable 3D space rather than a flat tree. The aim is not to force order onto the chaos, but to show the chaos — so the connections that were always there become visible. Status Live at jarvis-i-snowy.vercel.app — open and usable. Independent project; built end-to-end.

OA-GNN-SC — Overlap-Aware Speaker Diarization

Active researchGraph Neural NetworksCo-author
+
Problem Diarizing overlapping speech — telling apart who is speaking when multiple voices co-occur — remains one of the harder open problems in speech processing. Existing pipelines often degrade sharply at overlap, and modern neural architectures tend to be too computationally expensive for resource-constrained or privacy-sensitive deployment. Approach A self-contained, end-to-end framework combining adaptive energy-based voice activity detection, overlap-aware k-NN graph construction, GraphSAGE-based embedding refinement trained with self-supervised contrastive loss, and SC-pNA spectral clustering with eigengap-based speaker count estimation. Result Evaluated on 35 sessions of the AMI Meeting Corpus, the framework achieves a mean Diarization Error Rate of 41.8%. Error decomposition shows the clustering and embedding stages are competitive — the dominant gap is the energy-based VAD front-end, which motivates future neural-VAD integration. Role Active contributor under the supervision of Dr. Riya Sil, Associate Professor, Brainware University. Working across the full research pipeline: formulation, architecture design, experimentation, result analysis, and academic writing.

Enhancing Clinical Reliability in Diabetic Retinopathy Detection

First authorSoCTA 2025Abstract published
+
Problem Diabetic retinopathy is among the leading causes of preventable blindness globally. CNN-based classifiers can match expert ophthalmologists in benchmark performance, but real-world deployment remains limited by noisy fundus images, poor interpretability, unstable behaviour on borderline cases, and class imbalance. Approach A Hybrid Fuzzy-Optimized Deep Learning Framework — a pretrained CNN extracts class probabilities from fundus images; a Fuzzy Inference System with rule-based decision correlation refines borderline predictions to reduce diagnostic uncertainty; a metaheuristic optimiser tunes both CNN hyperparameters and fuzzy membership parameters jointly. Result Evaluated on the EyePACS dataset: 96.2% accuracy, weighted F1 of 95.9%, weighted precision of 95.8%, weighted recall of 96.2%. Higher precision and fewer false positives than baseline CNN — important for clinical screening where overcalls erode trust. Designed to remain lightweight enough for resource-limited screening contexts. Venue Presented and abstract published at the 10th International Conference on Soft Computing: Theories and Applications (SoCTA 2025), Dr B R Ambedkar NIT Jalandhar, December 27–29, 2025. Full paper currently under peer review.
